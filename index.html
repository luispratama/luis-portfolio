About Me

I’m a final-year Mechatronics Engineering (Honours) student at UTS who enjoys building simple, robust systems that work in the real world. I specialise in mechanical building design using SolidWorks and have hands-on experience with Arduino, servo motion, and design for manufacturing.

Through my internship at Optik Consultancy / Byron Bay Technology, I designed a multidirectional nozzle housing with auto shut-off and integrated servos and Arduino for automation. This work sharpened my attention to tolerances, fit, and practical prototyping.

I care about the human side of engineering—how automation impacts people, not just throughput. I aim to balance efficiency with social impact and to design systems that are effective and considerate of users and workflows.

Reflection

Sustainability in Engineering

While designing a nozzle housing with multidirectional motion and auto shut-off, I initially focused on performance and reliability. Reflecting afterward, I recognised how automation can reduce manual work and change roles around a system. Responsible engineering means considering not only technical success but also the social impacts of our design choices.

This experience reframed sustainability for me as both technical and human. I aim to evaluate outcomes on users and workflows, not just throughput, and to bring an ethical lens to future decisions.

Global & Intercultural Practice

As an international student in Australia, I’ve seen how team dynamics are shaped by culture and comfort zones. Positive, inclusive teams improved our results and confidence. Going forward, I’ll take initiative to create respectful, supportive spaces so every member can contribute fully—skills that matter as much as technical tools in global engineering practice.
