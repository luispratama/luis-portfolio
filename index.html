Luis Pratama

Final-year Mechatronics Engineering (Honours), UTS · Sydney

Hi, my name is Luis Pratama, and I’m a final year Mechatronics Engineering student at the University of Technology Sydney, graduating in 2025. I’ve always been curious about how things work and how they can be improved. One of my earliest memories of this was being amazed at how an automatic toilet flushed by itself. It sounds simple, but to me, that small moment sparked a lifelong interest in engineering and automation.

Since then, I’ve been drawn to solving real world problems through design and technology. I enjoy building things that not only function but also feel smart and efficient. In my recent university project, I led the development of a robot perception system that detects and sorts objects using computer vision. Seeing the robot respond accurately based on something I programmed gave me a real sense of achievement.

As an international student, I’ve had both good and challenging experiences working in diverse teams. I’ve learned to speak up, adapt, and keep going even when things get tough. In the next few years, I hope to work in mechanical design or a technician role at a company like Tesla, where I can keep learning and contribute to projects that matter. One day, I’d love to start my own engineering consultancy and help bring smart ideas to life.

What motivates me most is seeing something I built being used and knowing it made someone’s life a little easier.
